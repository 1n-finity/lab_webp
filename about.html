About Me

I am Dr. P. Muthu Austeria, Assistant Professor in the Department of Chemistry at Jain University, Bangalore. My research focuses on computational catalysis discovery involving both molecules and materials, with an emphasis on bridging theoretical predictions with experimental applications through interdisciplinary approaches.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computational Chemistry from Pondicherry University in 2016, followed by extensive postdoctoral research experience at prestigious institutions including the Indian Institute of Science (Bangalore), Jeonbuk National University (South Korea), and KENTECH (South Korea).

My research utilizes Density Functional Theory (DFT) and other computational methods to address complex challenges in catalysis, energy storage, gas sensing, and materials design. I am committed to developing innovative solutions for energy devices, 2D materials, and MOFs through theoretical insights combined with experimental validation.

Research Philosophy

My approach to computational chemistry emphasizes an interdisciplinary methodology crucial for addressing complex research challenges in catalysis discovery. By leveraging Density Functional Theory (DFT) and other computational methods, I aim to complement experimental investigations and provide insights into the electronic reasons behind observed phenomena.

I am committed to bridging the gap between theoretical predictions and experimental applications. My research philosophy centers on utilizing computational methods to solve experimental challenges, accelerate discovery through property prediction, and design novel materials with tailored properties for energy storage, sensing, and catalysis applications.
