Join Our Research Group

Our computational chemistry research group at Jain University welcomes motivated students and researchers who are passionate about understanding molecular systems through theoretical and computational approaches. We offer exciting research opportunities across multiple areas of computational chemistry.

Current Opportunities

Ph.D. Positions

We are actively recruiting Ph.D. students interested in computational chemistry, quantum chemistry, molecular dynamics, and related areas. Successful candidates will have the opportunity to work on cutting-edge research projects and develop expertise in advanced computational methods.

What We Offer:

  • Access to high-performance computational resources
  • Training in state-of-the-art computational methods
  • Opportunities for international collaboration
  • Support for conference presentations and publications
  • Mentorship and career development guidance

Master's Research Projects

M.Sc. students seeking thesis research projects are welcome to join our group. Projects are available in various areas of computational chemistry, tailored to student interests and career goals.

Project Areas Include:

  • Molecular property prediction using quantum chemistry methods
  • Computational studies of chemical reactions
  • Electronic structure calculations
  • Photochemical process modeling

Undergraduate Research

Motivated undergraduate students interested in computational chemistry are encouraged to join our research activities. We offer summer research internships and semester-long research projects.

Benefits for Undergraduates:

  • Hands-on experience with computational chemistry software
  • Introduction to research methodology
  • Preparation for graduate studies
  • Potential for co-authorship on publications

What We Look For

Academic Background

  • Strong foundation in Chemistry, Physics, or related fields
  • Good academic record with analytical thinking skills
  • Prior exposure to physical chemistry or theoretical topics (preferred)
  • Interest in computational and theoretical approaches

Technical Skills

  • Basic programming experience (Python, MATLAB, or similar)
  • Familiarity with Linux/Unix systems (advantageous)
  • Mathematical background including calculus and linear algebra
  • Willingness to learn new computational tools and methods

Personal Qualities

  • Strong motivation and curiosity for scientific research
  • Ability to work both independently and collaboratively
  • Good communication skills in English
  • Persistence and attention to detail

Research Training & Development

Students joining our research group will receive comprehensive training in both theoretical concepts and practical computational skills. Our training program is designed to develop well-rounded computational chemists.

Theoretical Foundation

  • Quantum mechanics and electronic structure theory
  • Statistical mechanics and thermodynamics
  • Molecular orbital theory and chemical bonding
  • Spectroscopy and photochemistry

Computational Methods

  • Density functional theory (DFT) calculations
  • Molecular dynamics simulations
  • Ab initio quantum chemistry methods
  • Data analysis and visualization techniques

Professional Skills

  • Scientific writing and publication
  • Presentation and communication skills
  • Project management and time management
  • Collaboration and teamwork
